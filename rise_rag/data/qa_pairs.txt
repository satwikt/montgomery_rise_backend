---
Q: What is RISE?
A: RISE stands for Revitalization Intelligence and Smart Empowerment. It is a decision-support tool built for the City of Montgomery, Alabama, that takes city-owned vacant parcels and recommends the highest-impact land reuse options using real data. RISE combines live foot traffic data from Montgomery's ArcGIS system, flood risk data from FEMA, community distress signals from 311 service requests, and active federal grant windows to score each parcel across 8 dimensions. Google Gemini 2.5 Pro then generates plain-English recommendations. RISE was built for the WWVibes Hackathon at Alabama State University.

---
Q: What are the three hero parcels and where are they?
A: The three hero parcels are: Parcel A on Bibb Street, Montgomery AL 36104 (8.34 acres, Heritage story — 0.167 miles from Rosa Parks Museum); Parcel B on Upper Wetumpka Road, Montgomery AL 36130 (2.43 acres, Digital Infrastructure / IX Hub — 0.214 miles from MGMix internet exchange node); and Parcel C at 1600 Terminal Road, Montgomery AL 36108 (6.20 acres, Community Resilience and Food Access — in the USDA-confirmed food desert neighbourhood of Maxwell Heights / Gibbs Village).

---
Q: What is the heritage boost and why does it matter?
A: The heritage boost is a scoring mechanism that adds extra weight to recommendations for parcels near Montgomery's civil rights landmarks — specifically the Rosa Parks Museum (+25pt boost, 0.5-mile radius) and The Legacy Museum (+20pt boost, 0.5-mile radius). If a parcel is within 0.5 miles of these landmarks, the boost fires automatically. The result is that cultural, community, and public space uses rank higher than purely economic uses near civil rights landmarks. This is intentional — RISE was designed to reflect Montgomery's values, not just market logic.

The Legacy Museum is also approximately 0.18 miles away, adding potential additional heritage boost. Heritage score is approximately 78 out of 100, contributing approximately 19.5 points to the final score of 74.

Q: What is Parcel C's story and what is the urgency?
A: Parcel C at 1600 Terminal Road in the 36108 ZIP code (Maxwell Heights / Gibbs Village) is in a USDA-confirmed food desert. 32% of residents experience food insecurity, the poverty rate is 35%, and the nearest full grocery (Piggly Wiggly / Food Outlet on West Fairview Avenue) is 1.4 miles away — effectively inaccessible on Montgomery's 60-minute bus schedule. The USDA REDLG Q4 grant covers 75% of costs for a community grocery co-op here, meaning the city's out-of-pocket on a $500,000 project is only $125,000. The Opportunity Zone designation means private investors can fund the match. USDA VAPG is also open at 39 days remaining. At 6.20 acres, the parcel can accommodate the grocery, a health clinic, and an urban farm simultaneously.

Q: Where does the foot traffic data come from?
A: Foot traffic data comes from Montgomery's own ArcGIS dataset called Most Visited Locations. This is the city's tracked data on which named locations across Montgomery are actually visited, with recorded visit counts per location. RISE queries this dataset using a 1-mile radius spatial query around each parcel. The data is free, requires no API key, and is the city's own measurement — more credible than scraped third-party data for grant applications and city planning presentations.

---
Q: What is MGMix and why does it matter?
A: MGMix is the Montgomery Metropolitan Internet Exchange — a facility where internet service providers and large technology companies connect their networks locally. Having an IX node means data does not route through Atlanta or Birmingham. This has attracted Meta's $1.5 billion data centre to the Montgomery metro and created local demand for technology workers. Parcel B at 0.214 miles from MGMix is ideally positioned for a workforce training hub serving this demand.

---
Q: What is the 311 distress score?
A: The 311 distress score measures community complaint density near a parcel based on service requests filed in the last 90 days. 311 is Montgomery's non-emergency service line — residents call it to report overgrown grass, illegal dumping, abandoned vehicles, and other neighbourhood issues. RISE scores this 0–10, with higher scores indicating higher urgency for intervention. If more than 30% of calls are about vacant-lot conditions specifically, a +2 bonus fires. Parcel C scores 7 out of 10 because the 311 call pattern directly reflects complaints about the neglected vacant land at 1600 Terminal Road.

---
Q: What is the flood risk score?
A: The flood risk score indicates whether a parcel falls within a FEMA flood hazard zone, scored 0–5 where 5 is lowest risk. RISE queries Montgomery's OneView Flood Hazard Areas layer for FEMA National Flood Hazard Layer data. Zone X scores 5 (minimal risk), Zone AE scores 0 (high risk, expensive to develop). All three hero parcels score 5 out of 5 — they all fall in FEMA Zone X.

---
Q: How does RISE use Gemini AI?
A: After computing all 8 dimension scores, RISE sends a structured prompt to Google Gemini 2.5 Pro containing every score with its explanation, ArcGIS foot traffic data, open grant windows, and community health flags. Gemini generates three plain-English land reuse recommendations explaining the data — it does not recalculate scores. RISE uses Gemini's JSON mode for clean, parseable output. Total API cost for all three parcels is approximately $0.004. If no Gemini key is configured, RISE falls back to pre-written zone-context recommendations.

---
Q: Is the data in RISE real?
A: Yes. Foot traffic comes from Montgomery's live ArcGIS Most Visited Locations endpoint. Flood risk comes from FEMA's National Flood Hazard Layer via Montgomery GIS. 311 distress data comes from Montgomery's actual 311 records. Grant deadlines and coverage percentages come from Grants.gov and USDA programme documentation. Parcel coordinates, acreage, and ownership come from Montgomery GIS, reverse-geocoded and confirmed on March 7, 2026 using reverse_geocode_check.py. Health statistics come from USDA ERS, PIH 2024, and ACS 2023 5-Year data for the specific census tracts. Every number is traceable to a public source.

Q: What track is RISE built for?
A: RISE was built for the Workforce and Economic Growth track at the WWVibes Hackathon at Alabama State University. It also has cross-coverage with the Smart Cities track (ArcGIS integration, data-driven planning) and the Civic Access track (community health equity, food desert intervention, heritage preservation). The scoring model reflects Montgomery's specific economic development priorities: civil rights heritage, technology workforce development, and food access equity.

Q: Can RISE scale to all of Montgomery's 200+ vacant parcels?
A: Yes. The scoring engine in rise_selector_v3.py runs on any number of parcels with the same 8-dimension model. ArcGIS, Flood Hazard, and 311 sources are all free with no documented rate limits at the relevant query volumes. Gemini at the free tier supports 500 requests per day — scoring 200 parcels requires 200 Gemini calls, fitting within the free tier in a single run.
