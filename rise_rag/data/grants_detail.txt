DOCUMENT: Grant — USDA Rural Economic Development Loan and Grant (REDLG) Q4
PARCEL_ID: general
TOPIC: grant_usda_redlg

Grant name: USDA Rural Economic Development Loan and Grant Programme (REDLG), Q4 2026 cycle.
Status: OPEN as of March 8, 2026.
Deadline: approximately July 1, 2026 — 115 days remaining from March 8, 2026.
Administering agency: United States Department of Agriculture, Rural Development division, Alabama state office (located in Montgomery — the city has a direct relationship with programme officers).

What it funds: Economic development projects in rural and low-income communities. Eligible uses include community facilities, grocery and food access infrastructure, workforce training facilities, broadband infrastructure, and small business development.

Coverage amounts by parcel:
- Parcel A (Bibb Street, 36104): up to 70% of total project costs. On a $500,000 heritage plaza: USDA covers $350,000, city contributes $150,000.
- Parcel B (Upper Wetumpka Road, 36130): up to 70% of total project costs. On a $500,000 training facility: USDA covers $350,000, city contributes $150,000.
- Parcel C (1600 Terminal Road, 36108): up to 75% of total project costs. The ZIP 36108 median income of $29,400 and poverty rate of 35% qualify for the 70–75% top coverage tier. On a $500,000 grocery co-op: USDA covers $375,000, city contributes $125,000. On a $1,000,000 project: USDA covers $750,000, city contributes $250,000.

Match requirement: Applicant must cover the remaining 20–25% of project costs. This can come from city general funds, other grants, private donations, or Opportunity Zone investment.

Eligible applicants: Local governments, non-profit organisations, federally recognised tribes. The City of Montgomery is a direct eligible applicant.

Application process: Submitted through Grants.gov and the USDA Rural Development Alabama state office in Montgomery.

URL: rd.usda.gov/programs-services/business-programs/rural-economic-development-loan-grant-program/al

DOCUMENT: Grant — USDA Value Added Producer Grant (VAPG)
PARCEL_ID: general
TOPIC: grant_usda_vapg

Grant name: USDA Value Added Producer Grant (VAPG).
Status: OPEN as of March 8, 2026.
Deadline: approximately April 16, 2026 — 39 days remaining from March 8, 2026.
Administering agency: USDA Rural Development.

What it funds: Planning and working capital grants for projects that add value to agricultural products or create new market opportunities. In the context of RISE, this funds community food production, processing, and distribution infrastructure — including urban farms, community market build-outs, and food co-op feasibility planning.

Coverage: up to 50% of total eligible project costs.
Match: 1:1. For every dollar of USDA funding, the applicant must contribute one dollar.

Cost example: A $300,000 urban farm and market build-out on Parcel C — USDA covers $150,000, community partner or Opportunity Zone investor covers $150,000.

Best fit: VAPG is best suited to Parcel C's Urban Farm and Market recommendation and could support Parcel A's Community Cultural Centre and Vendor Market if a food or market component is included.

At 39 days remaining, VAPG is more urgent than REDLG Q4. Teams pursuing both grants must prepare two separate applications on an overlapping timeline.

URL: rd.usda.gov/programs-services/business-programs/value-added-producer-grants-23

DOCUMENT: Grant — HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG)
PARCEL_ID: general
TOPIC: grant_hud_cdbg

Grant name: HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG).
Status: Formula entitlement — Montgomery receives this funding annually without a competitive application.
Administering agency: US Department of Housing and Urban Development.

What it is: CDBG is a formula-based federal grant distributed to entitlement communities. Montgomery qualifies and receives an annual allocation automatically — approximately $2–3 million per year based on historical HUD data.

What it funds: Acquisition of real property, rehabilitation of residential and non-residential structures, construction of public facilities including grocery stores, clinics, and community centres, and economic development. All three hero parcel development types are eligible CDBG uses.

How RISE connects to CDBG: CDBG can be used as the match for USDA REDLG Q4, potentially reducing the city's direct cash contribution further. Using CDBG as a 20–25% match on a USDA-funded Parcel C grocery co-op means the city's general fund expenditure approaches zero.

DOCUMENT: Grant — Opportunity Zone Tax Incentive
PARCEL_ID: general
TOPIC: opportunity_zone

Programme name: Opportunity Zones (Qualified Opportunity Zone, QOZ).
Type: Federal tax incentive — no application deadline, no competitive process.
Administering agency: US Treasury / IRS.

What it is: Private investors who place capital gains into a Qualified Opportunity Fund (QOF) can defer and reduce capital gains tax liability when the fund invests in businesses or real estate in a designated QOZ census tract.

Relevance to RISE: The census tract containing Parcel C at 1600 Terminal Road, 36108, is a federally designated Opportunity Zone. Private investors — real estate developers, impact investors, community development finance institutions — receive significant tax benefits for investing in development here. This attracts private equity to the community grocery co-op or urban farm without requiring the full match to come from public funds.

How it reduces the city's burden: If an Opportunity Zone fund provides the 20–25% match for a USDA REDLG Q4-funded Parcel C grocery co-op, the city's direct cash contribution from general funds approaches zero. The investor receives tax benefits; the community receives the grocery store.
